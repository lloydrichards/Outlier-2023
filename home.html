Hosted by the Data Visualization Society, Outlier is an annual conference to celebrate the global visualization community. We see this event as an opportunity to think outside the box and make something unique and different. For the first time, we will have a hybrid event: in-person in Porto, Portugal, and virtual.

Connect with the community

Outlier is all about connections: online or in-person you will find many opportunities for networking and connecting with the dataviz community in a variety of ways.

Learn from our speakers and experts

The field of data visualization includes people of many backgrounds, geographies, languages, and experience levels. We aim to secure a diverse lineup of speakers covering a wide range of topics to leave you feeling excited and inspired.

For the first time, Outlier will also offer the possibility to attend in-person or virtual workshops (DVS MasterClasses at Outlier) to enhance your skills.

Inspire others

Want to host a Q&A, moderate a panel, run a discussion on a topic, or even host a game session? No problem! In addition to the curated talks, everyone will have an opportunity to plan unconference sessions, creating experiences by and for those in attendance.

Outlier does not follow a specific topic, but this year we are giving particular attention to:

  • Inclusivity and Accessibility
  • Business Intelligence and Analytics
  • Infographics and Data Storytelling
  • Data Art and Innovation
  • Data Literacy, Data Culture and Data Teams

Porto

Porto is a lovely city in the North of Portugal, the second largest in Portugal, but obviously the best if you ask locals (just kidding! … Better to find that out by yourself)

How to get here?

Flying is still the best way to reach Porto from abroad. You can also travel by train or by car (even though parking is tricky if your accommodation does not have a parking spot).

Getting to and from the airport

You can easily get to the airport or to the city centre by metro. Line E (Purple) runs every 20 or 30 minutes, depending on the time and day of the week. Know more about other options here.

Where to stay?

There are plenty of hotels, hostels, and Airbnb options. Walking is always a good option, but the streets can be steep, so we recommend wearing comfortable shoes. Taxis and Ubers abound as well as buses and metro stations.

What to do?

Eat. Well, there are plenty of other things to do, but Portuguese people love to eat and drink. Some nice recommendations here.

Concerns

The city is mostly safe, you just need to be careful with your belongings, especially in more touristic areas such as Ribeira.

Some areas are under construction due to the metro expansion, so you might need to take some detours to get to where you want to go.

Weather in May is usually good - sunny and warm, but with colder nights. It does rain a lot in Porto, but usually not in May.

COVID-19 restrictions

COVID-19 travel restrictions were lifted in Portugal. Providing proof of vaccination, recovery from COVID-19 or negative test results is not required. Know more.

Event safety and inclusivity

Like all DVS-hosted events, Outlier attendees are expected to abide by our Code of Conduct to ensure a welcoming and inclusive event for all attendees. This extends to attendees joining in person, and virtually.

We are mindful of the continued concerns around COVID-19 and are committed to creating a safe event for attendees who opt to join in person. Here is what you can expect if attending in person:

  • Attendees are encouraged to be up to date on their COVID-19 vaccinations, including the most recent bivalent booster.
  • If you have a positive COVID test that unfortunately prevents you from attending, we will give you the option to have your ticket price refunded.
  • Masks are not required. We ask that attendees be kind and respectful of others, particularly those who are opting for different levels of protection from yourself.

We encourage data visualization enthusiasts to join the event the way they feel most comfortable.

The agenda will be available a few weeks before the event. Keynote speakers will be announced shortly.
